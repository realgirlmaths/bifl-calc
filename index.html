How this works: The breakeven price is the present value of all future cheap purchases, where each year's cost grows with inflation and is discounted at your return rate (growing annuity PV). If you enter resale inputs, the PV of the expected resale proceeds is added to the breakeven ceiling — because you'll recover that money at end of life. The resale value is computed as actual price × (1 + appreciation)^lifespan × retained %, then discounted back to today. The effective cost line shows the quality item's real outlay net of resale.
