Cumulative cheap cost (nominal) Breakeven price Actual quality price
How this works: The breakeven price is solved so that the quality item's net cost (purchase price minus the present value of its resale proceeds) equals the present value of all future cheap purchases (a growing annuity discounted at your return rate). Resale proceeds are the breakeven price appreciating at the item's annual rate over its lifespan, multiplied by the retained value fraction, discounted back to today. Because both sides scale with price, the equation resolves to a closed-form: P = annuityPV / (1 − retain × ((1+appr)/(1+rate))^n).
